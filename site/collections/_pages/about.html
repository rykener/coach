About me

Hi. I'm Eleanor, but some people online know me by my other name, Lithium. I write poetry, an advice column, silly satire, and sometimes more serious essays that I am compiling for a book. I make memes, I'm disabled and queer/trans, and I contemplate a lot about how to engage in the coaching industry with integrity. I have been practicing Zen Buddhism for most of my adult life. I live in San Francisco.

When I graduated high school I made the decision that college debt wasn't worth the trade off of an undergrad degree. I vowed to always learn how to operate on the allowable margins. I worked jobs as a pizza maker, a watchmaker, an electronics repair technician, chief of operations of a startup, a software engineer, and a Zen Buddhist monk. After I got really sick I realized how important it is to me that I spend my life helping people. I contemplated working as a chaplain or even as a minister, but unfortunately there are very few helping professions of that type that do not require a masters degree. Living in SF and putting myself through 7 years of school is financially unrealistic. This is where the coaching comes in.

I believe my deep skepticism of the coaching industry and my sincere desire to engage in this work with the utmost integrity sets me up to be able help my clients...

My health journey

I started experiencing chronic pain when I was 12 years old and for the most part that's been my experience ever since. About 10 years later I was in a vehicle collision that not only turned up the dial on how severe my pain was, but it also left me profoundly fatigued and with a host of other symptoms. For the next 8 years I saw doctor after doctor and I received bogus treatment after bogus treatment. I came to eventually understand for myself that I had ME/CFS but I was never able to get traction with the medical system on that.

Then I got covid and my bothersome (and yet still disabling) fatigue started to get worse and worse. One week I was struggling to walk to the end of my block, and the next week I was struggling to walk to my kitchen. I went to the emergency room and they told me I was a picture of perfect health. They recommended exercise. I spent the next 10 months experiencing what is known as "very severe" ME/CFS. I mostly laid horizontally in bed and lived off Soylent and the grace of others.

Somehow, one thing led to another and I found myself in the office of a NUCCA chiropractor who was just a few minutes from my apartment. I went into his office in an electric wheelchair. He took some x-rays of my head and neck and told me my "atlas" (the C1 vertabrae) was 6 times as far out of line as it needs to be to cause significant problems. I laid on his table and he did a small massage to my upper neck. After just two treatments like this I was fully walking again. After a couple months I had built most of my strength back.

This is a remarkable story, and is not normal. Not only am I lucky enough to have a version of ME/CFS that has some concrete treatment, but I was lucky enough to find that treatment and to respond to it. I do not expect most people with ME/CFS to find something similar... but we also can't know if we don't look.

Today I still live with a variety of disabling health issues, even though I have recovered from the worst of it. I am unclear if my system will ever be able to bounce back from all of the trauma of illness. I do not define my life by this.

My training

I am currently training as a mental health and wellness coach as well as a hotline crisis counselor. Once my training is complete I will be eligible to sit for the National Board for Health and Wellness Coaching (NBHWC) certification.

I have completed Mental Health First Aid training and I am certified to teach the Right Use of Power program. I have been practicing Zen Buddhism, in all its facets, for over a decade. I am not a Buddhist teacher, but my extensive training in mindfulness and compassion does inform my approach. I founded and facilitate a group for transgender Buddhists.
